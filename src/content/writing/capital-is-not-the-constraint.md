Every region that wants a deep tech industry reaches for the same lever first:
more capital. Another fund, another grant program, another co-investment
vehicle. It is the most visible lever and the easiest to announce, which is
exactly why it is rarely the binding one.

## What actually runs out first

Spend time with founders who are genuinely stuck and the shortage is almost
never dollars. It is a second technical co-founder. It is a contract
manufacturer who will take a small run seriously. It is the one person in town
who has taken a hard-tech company from prototype to production and lived to
describe the scar tissue.

Money is fungible and mobile. The other things are neither. A region can import
a cheque overnight; it cannot import a decade of operating experience.

## The harder, slower work

If that is right, then the work is less glamorous than a fund launch. It looks
like apprenticeship, dense networks, and a tolerance for the years it takes
those to compound. That is the work I am most interested in — and the reason
I am moving home to do it.
